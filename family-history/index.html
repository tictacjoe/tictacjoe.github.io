The Solana Line: Sephardic & Spanish Heritage

Traced to Alonso Solana, who arrived in St. Augustine from Spain in 1613. This branch consists of Sephardic Jewish (Conversos) who migrated from the Halevi lineage in Spain through Portugal and the Canary Islands.

  • Hidden Heritage: Transitioned to "Mary Games" in the 1700s after Maria Manuela Solana married James Games in Maryland.
  • Genetic Markers: Research links family G6PD deficiency and Mediterranean DNA markers to these Levantine origins.

The Stepp Family: Balkan Soldier Theory

Focuses on the Stepp (Stapp) line in the North Carolina mountains.

  • Balkan Roots: DNA indicates the E-M78/E-V13 haplogroup, common in modern-day Croatia.
  • Roman Connection: Suggests an ancestor may have been a Roman soldier from Dalmatia stationed in Britain.
