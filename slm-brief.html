Security Log Monitoring Platform

The Security Log Monitoring Platform (SLM) was a project built for CenturyLink, a global telecommunications company. The idea of the project back in 2014 was to leverage the growing acceptance of cloud-based infrastructure to provide a low-cost Security Information and Event Management Platform and Security Operations Center subscription with predictable monthly opex charges. The platform included the development of proprietary collection technology in the form of a virtual collector, as well as facilities for the efficient and inexpensive ingestion of big data streams, modular analysis and correlation functions, and modern, web based display and reporting technology. There were 2 major iterations of the platform. The first was built from elastic compute resources (storage, ram, and cpu) in the form of virtual machines and software defined networking. The second version was built on top of AWS infrastructure with heavy use of serverless lambda functions, allowing us to drastically reduce operating costs. The platform was part of a complete Managed Security Services offering which included assessments, incident response, program management, etc. During my employment with CenturyLink the SLM platform was launched in the U.S., Asia (Singapore), and Europe. The platform was also adopted as an internal tool by CenturyLink cyber security teams and is being integrated with various other security products offered and used by CenturyLink.
